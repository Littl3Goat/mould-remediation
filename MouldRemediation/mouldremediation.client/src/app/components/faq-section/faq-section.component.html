Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about mould removal and our services.

  • Mould can sometimes be easy to spot — dark patches on walls or ceilings, a musty smell in the air, or paint that's peeling or bubbling. But it's not always visible. If your home feels damp, lacks ventilation, or you notice persistent condensation on windows, these can all be early signs of mould. You might also notice allergy-like symptoms such as sneezing, coughing or irritated eyes, especially when indoors. Common trouble areas include bathrooms, kitchens, laundries, and anywhere moisture builds up without enough sunlight or airflow. If any of this sounds familiar, it's worth taking a closer look — mould can spread quickly if left untreated.
  • Yes, mould can pose serious health risks, especially when left untreated. It releases spores into the air that, when inhaled, can cause allergic reactions, asthma flare-ups, respiratory issues, and irritation to the eyes, nose or throat. People with existing conditions, young children, the elderly or those with weakened immune systems are particularly vulnerable. Even if you're generally healthy, long-term exposure to mould can still impact your wellbeing. If you're experiencing unexplained symptoms while indoors, mould could be part of the problem.

  • You can try to clean small patches of mould yourself using vinegar or mild cleaners, but it's important to know that surface cleaning often doesn't fix the root of the problem. Mould tends to grow into walls, ceilings, or hidden areas where moisture is present. Without proper treatment, it usually comes back. At our company, we don't just remove what you can see — our professionals identify the cause, treat the affected area thoroughly, and help prevent future growth. For your safety and long-term peace of mind, professional mould removal is always the smarter choice.
  • The key to preventing mould is controlling moisture. Make sure your home has good ventilation, especially in areas like bathrooms, kitchens and laundries. Use exhaust fans, open windows when possible, and avoid drying clothes indoors. Keep humidity levels low, fix any leaks quickly, and clean up water spills right away. It's also helpful to let sunlight into rooms and move furniture slightly away from walls to allow airflow.
