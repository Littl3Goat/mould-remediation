Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about mould removal and our services.

Common signs of mould include visible growth (often appearing as black, green, or white spots), a musty odor, water stains, peeling wallpaper, and health symptoms like allergic reactions, respiratory issues, or headaches when in the affected area. If you suspect mould, it's best to have a professional inspection.

Mould can cause health problems, especially for people with allergies, asthma, or weakened immune systems. Symptoms can include coughing, wheezing, nasal congestion, eye irritation, skin rashes, and in some cases, more severe reactions. Some moulds produce mycotoxins that can cause serious health issues with prolonged exposure.

The duration of mould removal depends on the extent of the infestation, the affected materials, and the size of the area. Small jobs might take 1-2 days, while larger or more complex jobs can take several days to a week. Our team will provide a timeline estimate after the initial inspection.

Small areas of mould (less than 10 square feet) on non-porous surfaces can sometimes be cleaned with proper precautions. However, larger infestations, mould on porous materials, or mould caused by sewage or contaminated water should always be handled by professionals. DIY removal can often spread spores and worsen the problem.

To prevent mould from returning: control humidity levels (keep below 60%), fix leaks promptly, ensure proper ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens, use dehumidifiers in damp areas, clean and dry water-damaged areas within 24-48 hours, and schedule regular mould inspections. Our team provides customized prevention plans after remediation.
